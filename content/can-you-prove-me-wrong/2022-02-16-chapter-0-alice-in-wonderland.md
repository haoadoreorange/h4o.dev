It was a someday afternoon in one of my primary school years, and every kids
were chatting about the coming holiday. I think that was the first time we've
ever talked about it, not that the first time we had it, it just never came up
as a suject. I remember going home that day talking to my mom and found out
surprisingly that we actually don't celebrate that holiday in the family. I like
to describe my family and relatives as a quote borrowing from Mr. Moustafa in
"The Grand Budapest Hotel":

> To be frank, I think his world had vanished long before he ever entered it -
> but, I will say: he certainly sustained the illusion with a marvelous grace!

They look at life and behave differently from what I see in the world. My
parents, my aunts and uncles aren't much like my friends's in a particular way.
The discussions we had on meet up occasions are not quite the same (which I
didn't found out until much later on). Their definition of right and wrong from
the old world sometimes straight up conflicts what I know, and I can tell you
they certainly protected theirs with a fine amount of effort and pride,
sometimes to an extreme level. I always find it fascinating that when we look
back at something that became too obvious now, it's hard to imagine that we
didn't understand it before. If you asked me back then, I couldn't say that
there was any conflict at all, I just kow vaguely, that something always feels
odd, something that I couldn't explain clearly.

This conflicting way of life is actually from my mother's side family only, and
not from my father's side. And I think in our small family, he balanced her out
somehow. That might be the reason why, from what I can still remember, after he
wasn't there anymore, the influence of my mother's side family way of life
became more and more prominent. That certainly did no good to me. A lof of
things I touched and saw, interacted with, even with the relatives on my
father's side, became much less fit whenever I went home or met with my mom's
brothers and sisters. I eventually didn't like being around them, I couldn't
understand their reasoning, I tried finding as much excuses as possible to stop
seeing them. That lasted way into my highschool years, at one point my grandma
made a promise to give me money, if I go there to see her, which only made me
feel even more awkward. In contrast, I did visit my father's side grandpa a lot,
I just feel more normal being around him.

I went on studying abroad in France after my highschool. During these 6 years
studying and working, I had these chronic "self sabotaging" phases, in which I
feel clueless, and stopped making efforts in life. It was always some thinking
that got in the way (now I can't remember what it was anymore), and at the end
of each phase, I thought I found some answers, which kinda lasted for a while.
During one good mood phase, on a night bus in Paris, I asked a friend what she
really thinks about me, she said: "You always say that you are free, but I don't
think you are really, you live by the rules, you just don't admit it". Well, I
call bullshit on that back then of course. I was feeling good, I was doing good,
I felt I was free to do anything I wanted, I felt she couldn't be more wrong. I
really don't know now if she was that wise or just lucky, but I gotta give her
credit for saying it out loud. I indeed lived very much by the rules. Back then,
I always convinced myself with a big purpose in life, give that being doing good
in life, taking care of my little brother, and million other things, I don't
know how or why I got some of those, I just now know that I was a TGV, going as
fast as I can to a destination. In 2019, I went back to my hometown for a long
period, hoping I to spend time with my family, the time that I'd lost during
those years abroad. In reality it turned out really really bad with countless of
problems on both side that I don't bother going in details here.

By the time I moved to Canada a while later, I'd almost stopped talking to my
brother entirely. Thinking about them got me angry, I could barely talk with my
mother, once in couple full moon, each time ending up in a fight that got me a
sleepless night. I made up my mind to cut ties with my family on both side. I
told myself it was a sinking ship, and better save myself than sorry, nothing I
did fixed anything anyway.

This opened the door to another "self sabotaging" phase, a crisis, for which I
didn't realize at first how big it was gonna be.
